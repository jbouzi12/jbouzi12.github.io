Kitchen v.s. Restaurant

Before beginning my Dev Bootcamp experience, I never truly sat down and contemplated how rigorous my upcoming experience will be. I typically try to set high goals for my self, create a flexible plan of action, and attempt to execute no matter the difficulty. If I have a goal in mind or if I want a certain type of experience I honestly do not care how challenging it may be. As a result I tend to not think about the gravity of such challenges; I'm not sure if this could be potentially bad in the future, but I'd like to think that this trait allows me to dive head first into challenges and really push myself to the best of my abilities.

Now enter Dev Bootcamp. Since beginning Phase-0 of the program I am slowly beginning to realize that this experience I am embarking upon may prove to be one of the most challenging journeys of my life. Yet, I can' even fathom what I will be able to accomplish as I take each step, closer and close to my goal of becoming a dynamic developer. I try not to think about Dev Bootcamp through the eyes of a consumer: I'm paying to learn various coding languages and eventually get a cool job. I am more concerned with what I will be able to accomplish and developing my ability to transform ideas into innovative products.

Despite my excitement, I would be lying if I said that I was not worried. My greatest concern is falling behind amidst such a fast-paced, dynamic environment. However, I am comforted by the fact that I will be able to receive guidance from amazing professors as well as my cohort-mates, many of whom already offer much support in our Google community. Hopefully, I will be able to thrive amidst the chaos and contribute to the cultivation of innovative ideas with my peers.



Jensen Bouzi, August 10, 2014
